Motivation

Following the completion of my BSc in Business Engineering and my current position at SBB, my interest and field of expertise has crystallized around data design and data visualization. Through the MSc program I aim to expand my knowledge in the data science field and gain all necessary skills to continue a successful career in the interface between people, data and design.

In my current role as an enterprise architect, I am often faced with the challenge of supporting the development of projects aiming at solving current issues while taking into consideration future changes. For this purpose, decision makers need to be made aware and convinced with a focused and conclusive communication. Today, we operate in an increasingly data-based and complex environment, which is often difficult to understand. Already during my relatively short time within the Enterprise Architecture Team at SBB, I can see that important projects are not further pursued or not even started due missing support from the management team. In my opinion this is often due to a lack of communication and poorly prepared data.

These challenges motivated me to pursue the DAS in Data Science, which I successfully completed this spring. Furthermore, with the CAS in DataViz I found my passion in the field of information and data visualisation. The interface between data, people and design has developed into my passion and chosen field of specialization. A deepening of the knowledge acquired during the DAS in Data Science is my motivation and ultimate goal for the completion of this master’s degree.

I am convinced that with the rising amount of data, we are more than ever required to combine data analysis skills with a creative data driven design approach to make better decisions. The MSc in Applied Information and Data Science best reflects my development goals and I would greatly appreciate the opportunity to expand my knowledge base through this program.
